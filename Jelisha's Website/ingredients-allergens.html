The nerdy bits

What's in 'em?

Exactly what the soap is made of, in plain English, plus an honest word about lye and essential-oil sensitivities. No mystery ingredients, no fine print.

The recipe

One base. Five scents. That's the whole trick.

Every single bar starts the same way: coconut oil and shea butter, turned into soap with lye (sodium hydroxide), then scented with essential oils. That's it. Only the essential oils change from bar to bar.

About the lye: it's how all real bar soap gets made. During saponification it reacts completely with the oils, and once the bars cure, there's no active lye left in the finished soap. The chemistry eats it. You're just left with a nice bar and a clean conscience.

Heads up

About those essential oils

Every bar is scented with essential oils, and essential oils are a common sensitivity for some folks. If you've got a known fragrance or essential-oil allergy, holler at Jelisha before you order, and she'll walk you through the scents so you land on one that treats your skin kindly.

Questions folks actually ask

Is there still lye in the finished soap?+

Nope, no active lye remains. It reacts completely with the coconut oil and shea butter during saponification, and after the bars cure there's none left in the finished soap.

What is the soap made of?+

Coconut oil and shea butter, saponified with lye (sodium hydroxide), and scented with essential oils. Every bar shares that same base.
